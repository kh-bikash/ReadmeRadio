# README Radio visual identity

## Style Prompt

README Radio uses a dark technical broadcast aesthetic: near-black canvases, restrained violet and blue signal glows, crisp geometric typography, and compact monospace metadata. Motion should feel like a responsive studio console—calm, purposeful, and synchronized to narration—rather than decorative.

## Colors

- `#070709` — primary canvas
- `#0B0B0E` — raised panels
- `#A78BFA` — active signal and focus
- `#60A5FA` — secondary data accent
- `#F5F3FF` — primary text

## Typography

- Geist Sans for interface and display copy
- Geist Mono for status, timestamps, and technical labels

## Motion

- Short spring entrances with soft deceleration
- Frame-driven pulses and progress indicators
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Keep narration and diagram emphasis synchronized

## What NOT to Do

- No rainbow gradients or unrelated accent colors
- No continuous browser-time animations in rendered video
- No glass effects that reduce text contrast
- No tiny labels or clipped architecture nodes
- No fake progress or decorative controls without behavior
